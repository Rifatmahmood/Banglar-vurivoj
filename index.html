Our Recipies

The cuisine of Bengal is as rich and distinct as the state itself.
The rule of Nawabs has deeply influenced Bengali food which is a combination of subtle and fiery flavours.

Kalavuna

Kala Bhuna (Bengali: কালা ভুনা, Chittagonian: হালা ভুনো, romanized: Hala Bhuno) is a famous dark and tender dish native to Chittagong prepared with shoulder pieces of beef and traditional spices; usually served during Mezban. It was originally served during the early 17th century. However, the origin of the dish is unknown. Bhuna (Chittagonian) means deep-frying; derived from Bhunna (Urdu) meaning to be fried and the word Kala means dark or black in Chittagonian.

Bhurta

Bhurtas are the simplest and the ultimate comfort food of Bangladesh served with rice. Bhortas are delicious, smooth, fiery and flavorful mashed goodies made with mustard oil, onions, garlic and red chilies. Most veggies or fish both fresh and dried can be mashed with oil, onions, garlic, chilies and occasional cilantro or lemon juice and turned into a bhurta.
